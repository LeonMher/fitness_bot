Back muscles

Warm-Up:
5-10 minutes of light cardio (e.g., jogging, jumping jacks) to increase heart rate and warm up muscles.
Back Exercises:
Pull-Ups:

Sets: 4 sets
Reps: 6-10 reps per set
Description: Hang from a pull-up bar, pull body up until chin reaches or clears the bar, lower back down with control.
Bent-Over Barbell Rows:

Sets: 4 sets
Reps: 8-12 reps per set
Description: Bend at the hips, keep back straight, pull barbell up to abdomen, lower back down with control.
Lat Pulldowns (or Assisted Pull-Ups):

Sets: 3 sets
Reps: 10-15 reps per set
Description: Sit at lat pulldown machine, pull bar down to chest level, squeeze shoulder blades together, release with control.
Dumbbell Rows:

Sets: 3 sets per arm
Reps: 10-12 reps per set
Description: Place one knee and hand on a bench, let the other leg hang, pull dumbbell up to rib cage, lower back down with control.
Core and Lower Back Exercise:
Hyperextensions:
Sets: 3 sets
Reps: 12-15 reps per set
Description: Lie face down on hyperextension bench, cross arms over chest, raise torso until hips are extended, lower back down with control.
Cool-Down:
5-10 minutes of stretching, focusing on the back muscles, to improve flexibility and aid in recovery.
Tips:
Form and Technique: Maintain proper form throughout each exercise to prevent injury and maximize effectiveness.
Progressive Overload: Gradually increase weights or reps as you become stronger to continue challenging your muscles.
Rest: Take 1-2 minutes of rest between sets to allow muscles to recover adequately.
Hydration and Nutrition: Stay hydrated and consume a balanced diet to support muscle growth and recovery.
